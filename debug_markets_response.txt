BRIEFING_START

The ASX 200 fell 1.51% to 8,744.4 in the prior session, its sharpest single-day move in recent weeks, with broad-based selling across financials, listed property, and technology leaving the benchmark well below its recent range. Wall Street's overnight recovery provides a constructive lead for today's open — the S&P 500 gained 0.84% to 7,398.9 and the Nasdaq surged 1.71% to 26,247.1, with large-cap technology names including Nvidia and Apple pushing to new record highs. The AUD/USD strengthened 0.57% to 0.7250, a positive signal for risk appetite that should translate to broader ASX support at the 10:00am open.

The dominant macro theme is US Federal Reserve rate timing and its implications for Australian yield-sensitive stocks. Bank of America now forecasts no Fed cuts until at least 2027, citing consecutive positive nonfarm payrolls and a labour market that removes urgency for near-term easing. The US 10-year yield fell to 4.36%, down 0.64% overnight, providing modest relief for the sectors hit hardest in the prior session — Westpac shed 2.93% to $37.44, NAB fell 2.91% to $38.36, and A-REITs Stockland and GPT Group each declined more than 3%.

Watch Westpac and NAB at open for stabilisation, with the softer yield backdrop the most direct catalyst for the two hardest-hit major banks from Friday's session. Copper's 1.98% overnight gain to $6.25 and gold at $4,720.40 provide a supportive environment for the materials sector — Newmont (NEM, $160.35) warrants attention given precious metals strength, and WiseTech (WTC, $42.27) may partially recover after its 4.63% decline given the Nasdaq's 1.71% overnight surge.
